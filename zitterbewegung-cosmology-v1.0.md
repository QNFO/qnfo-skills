## Abstract

The Zitterbewegung — the rapid oscillatory motion of elementary particles predicted by the Dirac equation — is not merely a quantum mechanical artifact but the fundamental mechanism by which spacetime acquires its metric structure. This research program develops the complete framework in six pillars: (1) the Dirac equation's Zitterbewegung as mathematical foundation, (2) the correspondence between Zitterbewegung cycles and ultrametric tree-depth steps, (3) the derivation of the FLRW metric and Einstein equations from aggregate Zitterbewegung dynamics, (4) dark energy as the zero-point Zitterbewegung energy with a predicted value of $10^{-123} M_{\text{Planck}}^4$ matching observations, (5) CMB log-periodic oscillations and p-adic non-Gaussianity as observational signatures of ultrametric cosmic structure, and (6) matter-antimatter asymmetry from chirality-dependent Zitterbewegung tree-depth evolution. Testable predictions are provided for CMB, large-scale structure, neutron EDM, and neutrino experiments.
